Our styles

Not sure which class to take? Find out a bit more about our class styles

Salsa Caleña is a special style of salsa that originates from the Colombian city of Cali, which is also known as the capital of salsa.

This style is famous for its lightning speed footwork that interprets all instruments of a song, while dancers maintain their upper body still and controlled.

This class will raise your heartbeat and challenge you physically and mentally, while you learn fast footwork combinations alongside traditional salsa music from Cali, Colombia.

Salsa On1, also known as LA style or crossbody salsa is distinguished by stepping forward on the first beat of the music with your left foot.

During social dances, couples usually break free to do salsa shines to interpret the parts of the songs that are more instrumental.

Our classes will be focusing on learning short ‘salsa shine’ routines. This will help you to improve your technique, timing and body movement so you’ll have more confidence dancing solo!

Bachata is a style of dance that originated in the Dominican Republic. It is characterised by its basic step of three steps, followed by a tap including a hip movement on the 4th beat.

Over the years the dance has evolved into many different styles: Bachata Sensual, Moderna, Fusion, Traditional and the original Dominican style.

Join us to learn footwork routines combining all of these styles or grab a partner and learn how to dance in our Bachata Partnerwork classes.

Our Latin Fitness classes have been specially designed by La Sucursal Dance - we like to think of it as our own version of Zumba!

We will be combining elements of salsa, bachata, mambo, merengue and reggaeton to get your heart rate up and burn calories!

Join this high energy class if you want to have fun moving to Latin music and burn calories without even realising.

Ladies Style is a class for anyone wanting to develop their femininity in their salsa.

This class will focus more on drill movements and techniques to help you to develop your arm, hand styling and body movement.
